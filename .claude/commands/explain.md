**Explanation structure:**

1. **Purpose** — In one or two sentences, what is the overall goal of this code?

2. **Inputs & outputs** — What data goes in and what comes out? Include types where relevant.

3. **Step-by-step walkthrough** — Walk through the logic sequentially. For non-obvious operations, explain *why* they are done that way, not just *what* they do.

4. **Key concepts** — Call out any algorithms, design patterns, data structures, or language features that a reader needs to understand to follow the code.

5. **Edge cases & assumptions** — What assumptions does the code make about its inputs? How does it behave at the boundaries (empty input, zero, null, very large values, etc.)?

6. **Potential gotchas** — Highlight any subtle bugs, off-by-one errors, race conditions, or other issues worth noting.

7. **Summary** — Restate the purpose in plain English as if explaining to someone unfamiliar with the codebase.

Use concrete examples with sample inputs and expected outputs where they aid understanding.
